# Outreach Framework

## Purpose

This document provides the outreach framework used by linkedin-ai-coach.

The purpose of outreach is not to sell immediately.

The purpose of outreach is to:

- Start conversations
- Build relationships
- Create trust
- Open opportunities
- Generate partnerships
- Create professional connections

Successful outreach focuses on people first.

Sales and opportunities come later.

---

# Outreach Philosophy

Most outreach fails because people:

- Pitch too early
- Talk about themselves
- Use generic templates
- Ignore personalization
- Focus only on selling

Successful outreach focuses on:

- Relevance
- Personalization
- Curiosity
- Value
- Relationships

The goal is to create meaningful conversations.

---

# The Outreach Ladder

Every successful outreach follows a progression.

## Stage 1

Awareness

The person becomes aware of you.

---

## Stage 2

Recognition

The person sees your profile or content.

---

## Stage 3

Connection

You establish initial contact.

---

## Stage 4

Conversation

A dialogue begins.

---

## Stage 5

Trust

Credibility develops.

---

## Stage 6

Opportunity

A business or professional opportunity emerges.

Never skip directly to Stage 6.

---

# Outreach Principles

## Principle 1

Personalization matters.

People respond to relevance.

---

## Principle 2

Relationships before requests.

Trust comes first.

---

## Principle 3

Short messages perform better.

Respect attention.

---

## Principle 4

Focus on the recipient.

Not yourself.

---

## Principle 5

Value creates responses.

Always look for ways to help.

---

# Connection Request Framework

Connection requests should be:

- Professional
- Relevant
- Short
- Personalized

## Example

Hi Sarah,

I enjoyed your recent post about leadership and team growth.

I'd love to connect and follow your insights.

Best,
[Name]

---

# New Connection Follow-Up Framework

After connecting:

Do not immediately pitch.

Start a conversation.

## Example

Hi Sarah,

Thanks for connecting.

I've enjoyed your content on leadership.

Curious, what do you think is the biggest challenge managers face today?

---

# Conversation Starter Framework

Use:

### Shared Interests

### Industry Topics

### Recent Content

### Common Experiences

### Professional Challenges

The goal is discussion.

Not promotion.

---

# Value-First Outreach Framework

Before asking for anything:

Provide value.

Examples:

- Share a useful resource
- Recommend an article
- Offer feedback
- Introduce someone
- Share relevant insights

People remember those who help them.

---

# Partnership Outreach Framework

Purpose:

Build relationships with complementary professionals.

## Example

Hi David,

I've been following your work in recruitment and appreciate your perspective.

I work with companies on employer branding, and I think there may be opportunities for us to exchange ideas and potentially collaborate in the future.

Would love to stay connected.

---

# Collaboration Outreach Framework

Purpose:

Create joint opportunities.

## Structure

Observation

Compliment

Idea

Invitation

## Example

Hi Jennifer,

I enjoyed your recent content about personal branding.

I believe our audiences share similar interests, and I think a collaborative discussion could provide value to both communities.

Would you be open to exploring ideas?

---

# Referral Outreach Framework

Purpose:

Generate introductions through trusted relationships.

## Example

Hi Michael,

I hope you're doing well.

I wanted to reach out because I'm currently helping service businesses improve their LinkedIn presence.

If you know anyone who could benefit from that, I'd appreciate an introduction.

Either way, always happy to help if I can.

---

# Recruiter Outreach Framework

Purpose:

Career opportunities.

## Example

Hi [Recruiter Name],

I came across your profile and noticed your work in recruiting.

I specialize in [Industry/Skill Area] and would love to connect and stay informed about future opportunities.

Looking forward to connecting.

---

# Thought Leader Outreach Framework

Purpose:

Build relationships with industry leaders.

## Strategy

1. Follow their content
2. Engage consistently
3. Add thoughtful comments
4. Share insights
5. Start conversations naturally

Do not immediately ask for favors.

---

# Re-Engagement Framework

Reconnect with inactive connections.

## Example

Hi Sarah,

It's been a while since we last connected.

I recently came across your profile again and noticed the great work you're doing.

How have things been on your side?

---

# Follow-Up Framework

Most opportunities require follow-up.

Recommended schedule:

### Follow-Up 1

3-5 Days

### Follow-Up 2

7-10 Days

### Follow-Up 3

14-21 Days

Keep follow-ups respectful.

Never pressure people.

---

# The Three Message Rule

If someone does not respond after three attempts:

Stop.

Move on respectfully.

Avoid persistence that feels intrusive.

---

# Lead Generation Outreach Framework

The objective is conversation.

Not immediate sales.

## Structure

Observation

Personalization

Relevant Insight

Question

## Example

Hi David,

I noticed you're helping manufacturing companies improve operations.

I've seen many businesses in that space struggle with visibility and lead generation.

Curious if that's something you're seeing as well?

Every meaningful conversation can create:

- Future opportunities
- Partnerships
- Referrals
- Friendships
- Business growth

Think long-term.

The best outreach is not transactional.

It is relational.

Relationships compound over time.
